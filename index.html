Things I do

I'm a researcher at the Autonomy, Agency, and Assurance Institute, at the Australian National University. I spend my time there reading, thinking, talking, and teaching about what's happening as we embed sensing, learning, communicating, and acting computers in everything else. Everyone's talking about cars, but what about medical devices? Trains? Elections? Your home?

I also volunteer for community organisations. I'm on the board of the ACT Conflict Resolution Service, and have held a variety of national roles in the Uniting Church including two terms on the Assembly standing committee.

I regularly contribute to open source software, which empowers users and invites them to become creators. Lately I've focussed on making software testing easier and much more effective. I'm a core developer of Hypothesis (and some extensions) and a Pytest maintainer. Python's scientific stack is starting to adopt property-based testing, which is very exciting!

I often speak about testing at conferences - for example an introduction to property-based testing at PyCon US, an expert deep-dive on 'Sufficiently Advanced Testing' at PyCon Australia, and a hands-on workshop.
